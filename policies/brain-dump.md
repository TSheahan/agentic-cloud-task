# Brain Dump

When the user holds substantial domain knowledge to transfer, agent-led
structured elicitation is the wrong default — it fragments the user's mental
model. **Brain Dump** inverts the direction of knowledge flow.

Before opening the dump, establish the **capture objective** — the form and
purpose the recompose output should take. The dump will arrive in narrative
form regardless of its content; the output is not always a narrative.
Declaring the objective upfront makes the recompose governable and gives the
user a shared reference for "done."

Three-phase sequence:

1. **Dump.** The user transfers raw domain knowledge. The agent's role is
   scribe: capture faithfully, ask only for clarification, do not
   restructure or analyse.
2. **Accumulate.** The agent tidies with minimal reordering — preserving
   the user's sequencing and mental model rather than imposing analytical
   structure. Gaps and tensions are noted but not resolved.
3. **Recompose.** A distinct cleanup phase after the user signals the dump
   is complete. The agent: discards very-low-signal elements, groups
   related topics that were scattered through the raw collection, and
   shapes the result toward the declared capture objective. This is where
   analytical reorganisation happens — separated from intake for two
   reasons: it avoids interfering with the user's transfer, and editorial
   decisions (what to discard, how to group) require the full body of
   material to be sound.

Any situation involving integration of large volumes of user knowledge or
source material benefits from the same dump-accumulate-recompose sequence.
